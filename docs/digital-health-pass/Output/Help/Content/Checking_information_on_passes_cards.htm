Checking information on health passes, cards

After you use the Digital Health Pass Verify app to scan credentials, cards, and passes, check both the color coding and the message displayed, for example, Valid or Invalid.

 

Table 11 Color coding on health passes, cards

A green symbol indicates that the pass or card is valid.

A red symbol indicates that the pass or card is invalid. To understand why the credential is invalid, check the accompanying status information.

 

Valid, invalid health passes and cards

To determine whether a holder's health pass or card is valid, here's what to check after scanning it.

Check the QR code's validity.

The holder must present the correct type of QR code, that is, presented by a valid issuer specific for healthcare-related use in digital wallets.

Determine if the pass is current or expired.

The validity time varies. Organizations can customize the time period. For example, for daily health checks, a QR code might be valid for a day. Or, for a vaccine, a QR code might be valid for a year. The issuer or customer uses business rules to define expiration.

Tip: If the pass you scan is expired, let the holder know. Ask them to confirm that they are sharing the correct pass.

Is the signature valid?

Make sure that the signature is current.

  • Valid signature: The associated certificate is trusted and not expired or revoked.

  • Invalid signature: Either the certificate has been revoked or the content signed has been altered.

Revocation status

Authenticated health pass administrators have the option of revoking (essentially, disabling) health passes and cards. For example, a testing lab might revoke a pass when, for instance, either if a problem occurred in a completed test or an error occurred in the lab results.
