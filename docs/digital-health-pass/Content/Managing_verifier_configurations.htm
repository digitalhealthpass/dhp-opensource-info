Managing verifier configurations

customers need to routinely update verifier configurations to stay current with verification requirements for healthcare credentials. Evolving healthcare events involve changing guidance, for example, from a company, school district, or a health authority, such as the CDC in the United States. In the greater population, with organizations and credential holders, there's a great deal of flux and on-going change. Challenges in staying current include, for example, available vaccines, credential types, and rules and policies affecting credential validity.

In a deployment, customers want to validate credential holders according to the latest guidance. When customers use the Verify app to scan and validate credentials, they want to use up-to-date criteria when determining whether a credential is valid (green) or invalid (red). administrators facilitate this by maintaining customers' verifier configurations.

Examples of common administration tasks

  • Update configuration values, for example, to change the validity time period for a vaccination
  • Enabling or disabling rules
  • Remove configurations from the configuration catalog

Caveats

Before proceeding:

  • Make sure that you understand what the customer wants to accomplish with the update.
  • Carefully consider any affect or impacts of your update.
