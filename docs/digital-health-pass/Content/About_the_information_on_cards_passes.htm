About the information on cards, passes

When you view a holder displays a credential for verification, as a verifier, you'll see several sections:  A summary, a QR code, and holder information. In addition to locating the QR code for scanning, you can check the credential information as needed to assist with verification.

The figure shows the credential sections that verifiers typically see. The dashed line approximates the area you'll see when scanning using a smartphone. To view all sections, scroll down the card or pass.

 

Figure: Information sections on health passes, cards

 

1. Summary section

This section includes:

  • The type of test, for example, a PCR test

  • The name of the issuer. This is provided by the issuer.

  • Date that the credential was issued, and the date that the credential expires

2. QR code

This section includes the credential's unique QR code, which you scan to verify. These are similar to QR codes that appear on product labels, but are specific for use in healthcare. For information about QR codes and healthcare standards, see About supported healthcare QR codes.

3. Holder information

This section includes information that varies, by category.
